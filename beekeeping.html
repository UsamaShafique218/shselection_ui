Media reports—including the ZDF program Frontal – Fakt— made it clear that a significant portion of imported honey is adulterated. According to estimates, up to 70% of the honey is stretched with cheap sugar syrup.

As a result, it loses its valuable, health-promoting enzymes, which make real honey so unique.

High-priced Manuka honey is also particularly affected. Due to its limited origin in New Zealand and low production volume, there is massive counterfeiting here— industry experts estimate up to 80% of the volume is counterfeit. In stores and online, these products are often sold at prices well over €100 per kilo.

Our research also shows that approximately six times as much Manuka honey is sold as is actually produced.

Only honeys that are certified by the New Zealand Beekeepers’ Association (UMFHA) and through the MPI DNA test, and whose origin is clearly traceable, offer reliable assurance. Given the extremely high margins in international trade, the incentive for counterfeiting is enormous.

GOX-certified honey represents a genuine alternative. It has comparable enzyme levels, is produced by carefully vetted beekeepers in Europe, and has a significantly lower carbon footprint due to short transport distances. Furthermore, it comes from healthy, sustainably managed bees.
